Don't take my word for it

Two things clients actually pay for: designing systems that hold, and making problems disappear. Here's the shape of both — patterns from real engagements.

01 · the architectA system that can't drop an order

The brief every e-commerce backend eventually faces — and the design I reach for. Watch an order travel through it.

2M orders/day 20× traffic spikes payments involved 99.95% uptime zero lost orders
orders-platform · system design shipped ✓
1Idempotency at the edge. Clients retry, networks flake — the same order submitted twice must charge once. Solved before it ever reaches business logic.
2Outbox pattern. The order row and its event commit in one transaction — no dual-write problem, no order that exists in the DB but never got processed.
3Failure is a feature. Payment provider down? Retries with backoff, circuit breaker, and a dead-letter queue you can inspect and replay — not a 3am mystery.
4Observable from day one. SLOs, traces and alerts designed with the system, not bolted on after the first outage.
